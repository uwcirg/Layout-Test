Clinical Topics Addressed by the HIV PROs

“What do we want to know about our patients that would allow us to do a better job—to increase our knowledge and take action?”
- Clinic Director, speaking about their implementation experience

PROs cover a broad range of topics clinically relevant to PLWH, including symptoms, health behaviors, basic needs, and social context. A resulting report summarizes results and displays topics of interest in order of clinical priority, and can depict changes over time. Alerts for critical issues such significant anxiety or distress, interference with function, or suicidal ideation, may be sent to an appropriate provider or displayed to the patient, depending on whether the patient is within or outside a clinical setting when using the PROs.

Evidence

The gathering and synthesis of patient-reported outcome (PRO) data for a clinical consultation has been shown to enhance joint decision-making; this includes improving the mutual understanding of difficult-to-assess patient factors such as mental health concerns, medication adherence and risky behaviours (e.g. substance misuse).

Implementation

PRO implementation involves several stages of planning and execution, as well as follow-up and assessment. The toolkit linked below has been designed to support PRO uptake in routine HIV care by highlighting key considerations for implementers; this includes implementation insights, example materials and testimonies from stakeholders who have first-hand experience with PRO implementation, covering key areas that need to be actioned or assessed. The objective is to facilitate easier and more effective PRO use where appropriate.

Impact

HIV PROs began as a way to enhance the information gathered as part of a multi site, HIV funded, HIV clinical research network. The PRO process proved to be of clinical value - telling clinicians – providing clinicians with information they needed to know about their patients, while they were seeing their patients, and while they were seeing their patients, in order to provide better and more comprehensive care. The PRO system has expanded to be part of the usual care of patients and many of these research partner organizations, and is now being used more broadly outside of that research network
