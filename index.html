Patient Reported Outcome measures, or PROs, elicit and assess the patient’s experience of clinically important issues, using brief, validated questionnaires. PROs provide the patient perspective of the effects of disease and treatment on well-being, function, symptoms, satisfaction with (and preference for) treatments, risky behaviors (such as substance abuse), and other outcomes. Clinical Topics Measured by PROs

“What do we want to know about our patients that would allow us to do a better job—to increase our knowledge and take action?”
- Clinic Director, speaking about their implementation experience

PROs cover a broad range of topics clinically relevant to people living with HIV, including symptoms, health behaviors, basic needs, and social context. A resulting report summarizes results and displays topics of interest in order of clinical priority, and can depict changes over time. Alerts for critical issues such significant anxiety or distress, interference with function, or suicidal ideation, may be sent to an appropriate provider or displayed to the patient, depending on whether the patient is within or outside a clinical setting when using the PROs.

Mental Health

Measures the mental health of a patient focusing on concerns like anxiety, distress, and suicidal ideation using frequency and severity.
(e.g., PHQ-9)

Substance Use

Measures frequency, quantity and type of patient substance use, including alcohol, nicotine and drugs.
(e.g., AUDIT-C, ASSIST)

Antiretroviral Adherence

Measures if and how regularly the patient takes antiretroviral medication and any barriers to their regular access.
(e.g., VAS, self-rating)

Sexual Risk Behavior

Measures frequency, types and use of protection in sexual interactions.
(e.g., HRAP)

Basic Needs

Measures if the patient’s nutritional, housing, and financial needs are being met.
(e.g., self-assessment)

Social Enviromment

[Social Determinats of Health?] Measures factors like social support, partner violence, and HIV related stigma in a patient's social environment.
(e.g., IPV-4, Stigma)

Body Change Distress

Measures the patient’s monitoring of the changes in their body as a result of HIV symptoms.
(e.g., FRAM)

Quality of Life

Measures how HIV has impacted the patient's quality of life and interference in function it has caused.
(e.g., EURO-QOL)

Symptom Tracking

Measures the patient’s HIV symptoms to better track and treat them.
(e.g., HIV Sx Inventory)

Evidence

The gathering and synthesis of patient-reported outcome (PRO) data for a clinical consultation has been shown to enhance joint decision-making; this includes improving the mutual understanding of difficult-to-assess patient factors such as mental health concerns, medication adherence and risky behaviours (e.g. substance misuse).

Implementation

Implementing PROs involves several stages of planning, execution, follow-up, and assessment. The toolkit linked below has been designed to support PRO uptake in routine HIV care by highlighting key considerations for implementers; this includes implementation insights, example materials and testimonies from stakeholders who have first-hand experience with PRO implementation, covering key areas that need to be actioned or assessed. The objective is to facilitate easier and more effective PRO use where appropriate.

This Patient-Reported Outcome (PRO) Implementation Toolkit is designed to provide practical advice to support the introduction of PROs in a clinical setting that supports the management of people living with human immunodeficiency virus (PLHIV). These insights are drawn from a range of sources, including published literature and primary interviews with stakeholders who have experience with integrating PROs into HIV clinical care.
