Evidence for PROs in Research Studies

The gathering and synthesis of patient-reported outcome (PRO) data for a clinical consultation has been shown to enhance joint decision-making; this includes improving the mutual understanding of difficult-to-assess patient factors such as mental health concerns, medication adherence and risky behaviours (e.g. substance misuse).

HIV PROs began as a way to enhance the information gathered as part of a multi site, HIV funded, HIV clinical research network. The PRO process proved to be of clinical value - telling clinicians – providing clinicians with information they needed to know about their patients, while they were seeing their patients, and while they were seeing their patients, in order to provide better and more comprehensive care. The PRO system has expanded to be part of the usual care of patients and many of these research partner organizations, and is now being used more broadly outside of that research network
